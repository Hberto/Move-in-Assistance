## Data Insights

1. Social and Mobility
   1. Transport infrastructure and population density
   - Based on the population in the district, the infrastructure of bike, bus and train could affect the decision where to live. Especially the infrastructure conditions for the use of a bike and framework around the bike is important for students since most students use the bike. On the other hand, students also use the public transportation which is included in the semesterticket. Is there more infrastructure for bikes in hight density areas or is it the other way around? 
2. Living conditions and life quality
   1. The cost of living keeps increasing due to inflation,  population and housing shortage. In the same time you want to live in a district where the life quality is high and the amount of offers for free time activities and education like public libraries is present. What are trade-offs? Are there any connections between the housing prices and the life qualtiy?
